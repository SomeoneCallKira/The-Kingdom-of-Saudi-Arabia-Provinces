The Eastern Province

The Eastern Province, known as Ash Sharqiyah in Arabic, is the largest region in Saudi Arabia, covering a diverse landscape that includes coastal areas along the Arabian Gulf and vast stretches of desert. Its capital, Dammam, along with Khobar and Dhahran, form a vibrant metropolitan area commonly referred to as the Dammam Metropolitan Area (DMA). This region is a vital economic hub, hosting a significant portion of the country's oil reserves and serving as a major center for the petroleum industry. The Eastern Province's strategic location and economic importance have led to a blend of modern infrastructure and cultural richness. In addition to its industrial significance, the region offers picturesque coastal areas, such as Half Moon Bay, providing residents and visitors with recreational opportunities. The Eastern Province's unique blend of economic dynamism, cultural diversity, and natural beauty makes it a crucial and multifaceted part of Saudi Arabia.
